BEREA COLLEGE NATIONAL SOCIETY OF BLACK ENGINEERS(BC-NSBE) Chapter Constitution

Article I

A. The name of this club shall be Berea College National Society of Black Engineers Chapter(BC-NSBE).

B. The purposes of the BC-NSBE is to foster programs that serve to stimulate and develop student interest in engineering; to strive to promote participation at all levels of responsibility in the field of engineering by the black communities, and to endeavor in the advancement of black professional engineers within the individual engineering disciplines at Berea College.

Article II

A. Membership and participation in this organization shall be free from discrimination of the basis of sexes, race, religion, ethnic group or national origin.

B. Only Berea College- National Society of Black Engineers student is eligible for voting membership in the BC-NSBE club.

C. Any Berea College faculty or staff member is eligible for non-voting membership in the BC-NSBE.

D. To be an active member, one must attend at least 50% of the club’s meetings throughout the year.

E. Membership shall require that the proper dues have been paid to both the National Society and this Chapter.

F. It is the policy of club not to discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, ethnicity, age, sex, physical or mental disability, pregnancy status, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, genetic information or covered veteran’s status in its admissions policies and all of its programs, activities or employment practices.

Article III

A. BC-NSBE Chapter shall elect the following officers: President, Vice President, Secretary and Treasurer who will be known collectively as the Executive Committee.

B. Officers must be full-time Berea College students. Only active BC-NSBE club members can be elected officers.

C. All officers will serve one-year terms. Officers may serve multiple terms, however, they must always meet the above conditions.

D. If in the judgment of the faculty advisor and the other members of the Executive Committee, an officer is no longer fulfilling the duties of his or her position, the officer shall be relieved from it, and an election shall be held within 10 school days to fill the position.

E. All officers shall be elected at the last official meeting of the spring semester of each year. The officers shall be elected by secret ballot (or a virtual equivalent). A simple majority will be required for elections. If no candidate wins a simple majority in the first round, a run-off election will be held between the two candidates who received the most votes in the first round. On election day, the advisor or an outgoing officer who is not in contention for an office will count the ballots.

F. Voting procedures (only members)

G. Membership Prerequisite (Membership Dues or certain criteria/Berea Engage)

  • duties of the President:
    • Shall preside over all meetings of this chapter and provide an agenda for each member at each regular meeting.
    • Shall serve as representative of this chapter.
    • Shall appoint and remove all chairpersons and members of all committees, except where otherwise provided for in this document, with the advice and consent of the Executive Committee.
    • Shall be responsible or designate someone to be responsible for reserving a place for each regular meeting during the academic year.

  • Duties of the Vice-President:
    • Shall preside in the absence or the inability of the President.
    • Shall perform such other duties as may be assigned by the President. If the office of the President becomes vacant for any reason during their elected term, the Vice President shall become the President until the President is able to return to the position or the term expires; thereby, creating a vacancy of Vice President.

  • Duties of the Secretary:
    • Shall keep accurate minutes of each meeting.
    • Shall keep an accurate record of membership and attendance at Chapter meetings.
    • Shall forward an annual report to the Executive Board of the National Society before February 1st, of each year, summarizing the activities of the Chapter during the year and listing the elected officers.
    • Shall keep all official records of this chapter except for those herein that the custody and control of shall be that of another office.

  • Duties of the Treasurer:
    • Shall keep an accurate account of the Chapter's funds.
    • Shall forward all National Society dues of the Chapter members.
    • Shall submit a report to the Chapter members at least once a month concerning the financial status of the Chapter, in writing.
    • Shall submit an annual report to the Executive Board of the National Society before February 1st of each year, giving a complete accounting of the Chapter's finances and reporting on such other matters as the Executive Board may require.
    • Shall be responsible for obtaining and/or maintaining a bank account in the name of this Chapter.
    • All monies received by the Treasurer shall be deposited in the Chapter's bank account, within five business days after such receipt, by the Treasurer.

  • Duties of the Membership Chair:
    • Shall coordinate events and other mechanisms for the recruitment of potential new members.
    • Shall process membership applications into the Chapter’s Database
    • Shall welcome and orient new members into the chapter
    • Shall focus on membership retention

  • Duties of the Programs Chair:
    • Shall lead the planning and execution of any national signature programs.
    • Shall render reports and accountings to the General Membership, the Chapter Executive Board, and National Office as required by the National Operating Guidelines.
    • Shall work with the Regional Programs Chairperson in order to develop programs in keeping with the goals of the National Society.

Article IV

A. Regular meetings shall take place no more than two weeks apart with the day and time determined by a three-fourths vote of the members present at the first meeting of the academic year.

B. The President may call an unscheduled meeting when necessary provided that every member receives a notification at least 24 hours prior to the meeting.

C. The first regular meeting date of the next academic year shall be decided at the last meeting of the present academic year.

Article V

A. A voting member shall be defined as a member enrolled as a student or affiliate member who is a current paid member of the National Society of Black Engineers both nationally and within the Chapter.

B. Any voting member may propose an amendment to this constitution.

C. A majority of voting members present shall be necessary for any business to take place except where noted in this document.

D. The Secretary will be responsible for updating the constitution.
